NCHS: Leading Causes of Death: United States

Ashley Toney | CS 498: Data Visualization - Summer 2019

Here, we'll aim to determine what the leading cause of death is in the United States and how it has changed over the past 18 years. This visualization aims to provide insight into the 10 leading causes of death in the United States as recorded over the past 18 years. The source of the data can be found at data.cdc.gov.


The Data

The dataset is provided by the National Center for Health Statistics and presents the age-adjusted deathrates for the 10 leading causes of death in the United States. These statistics are based on the underlying cause of death derived from US resident death certificates filed between 1999-2016.

I. Dimensions
  • Year: year of death (1999-2016)
  • Cause Name: underlying cause of death
  • State: state in which the death certificate was filed (includes each of the 50 states, DC, and the entire US)
  • Age-adjusted Death Rate: death rate adjusted to account for the aging population

II. 10 Leading Causes of Death
Alzheimer's disease Influenza and pneumonia
Cancer Kidney disease
Chronic lower respiratory diseases (CLRD) Stroke
Diabetes Suicide
Heart disease Unintentional injuries
